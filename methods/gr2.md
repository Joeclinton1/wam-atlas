 1074: Generalist Robot Manipulation. A long-standing goal in robotics research is to develop a generalist robot
 1075: agent that is able to accomplish a wide range of tasks in diverse environments. One of the most flexible
 1076: ways to specify tasks is through natural languages [15, 29, 5, 28, 30, 31, 27, 26, 32, 33, 34, 35, 36, 37].
 1077: Pioneering studies explored using large-scale robot datasets to learn generalist policies which are able to
 1078: accomplish a variety of tasks [32, 15, 29, 38]. To achieve generalization in unseen scenarios, some existing
 1079: works combined data from other domains with robot data in policy training [39, 5, 29]. Recently, a number
 1080: of works proposed to fine-tune a vision-language model which has been pre-trained on Internet-scaled data
 1081: to obtain robust and generalizable robot policies [28, 29, 40]. In addition, some recent works resort to 3D
 1082: information [41, 42, 43, 44] to achieve efficient policy learning by leveraging the geometry information
 1083: contained in 3D data. Another line of works proposed to condition the policy with a goal image instead of
 1084: a language [45, 46, 47, 48, 49]. And previous methods have also explored aligning the latent space of goal
 1085: images and languages to enable both goal image condition and language condition during training [27, 31, 33].

 1087: pre-trained on video generation with Internet-scale video datasets and then fine-tuned on robot data to predict

 1091: and robustness of policies [52, 53, 54, 55, 56, 57, 58, 40, 5]. A popular approach is to first learn useful
 1092: visual representations via masked modeling [53, 59, 60, 52] or contrastive learning [54, 61, 62, 63]. The
 1093: learned representations are then used for downstream policy learning. RPT [55] performed self-supervised
 1094: pre-training and showcased that pre-training with large robot datasets consistently surpasses training from
 1095: scratch. In reinforcement learning (RL), previous works proposed to first train a world model to obtain latent
 1096: state representations and then use them to train an RL agent [64, 56, 59]. VIPER [65] trained a video prediction

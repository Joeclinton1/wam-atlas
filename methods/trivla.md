 1284: COMPARISON METHODS
 1285: Generalist robot policies have been extensively investigated in prior research. In our experiments, we
 1286: select a representative subset of prior methods for comparison, focusing on those that have achieved
 1287: state-of-the-art performance or employ approaches similar to ours.
 1288: • RT-1 Brohan et al. (2022): A general action learning robot policy integrating semantic
 1289: features via Efficient-Net with FiLM-conditioning, subsequently employing token learners.
 1290: • Diffusion Policy Chi et al. (2023): A action learning approach modeling the robot’s visuo-
 1291: motor policy as a conditional denoising diffusion process enhanced with action diffusers.
 1292: • Robo-Flamingo Li et al. (2023b): A direct action learning policy leveraging a pre-trained
 1293: LLM, integrating visual information into each layer following the Flamingo Alayrac et al.
 1294: (2022).
 1295: • UniPi Du et al. (2024): Initiates by training a video prediction model for future sequence
 1296: generation and concurrently learns an inverse kinematics model between frames to infer
 1297: actions.
 1298: • MDT Reuss et al. (2024): Trains a diffusion transformer-based policy complemented by an
 1299: auxiliary MAE loss to facilitate future state reconstruction.
 1300: • Susie Black et al. (2023): Employs a fine-tuned InstructPix2Pix Brooks et al. (2023) model
 1301: to generate goal images and trains a downstream diffusion policy conditioned on these goal
 1302: images.
 1303: • GR-1 Wu et al. (2023): Models video and action sequences using an autoregressive trans-
 1304: former. During policy execution, GR-1 predicts one future frame followed by a action.
 1305: • Robo-Uniview Liu et al. (2024b): Develops a 3D-aware visual encoder supervised by a 3D
 1306: occupancy loss for policy learning.
 1307: • Vidman Wen et al. (2024): Pre-trained on the Open X-Embodiment video dataset, it em-
 1308: ploys a self-attention adapter to convert video representations into policies. However, Vid-
 1309: man’s performance is suboptimal due to the absence of fine-tuning the video model on
 1310: downstream tasks.
 1311: • Seer Tian et al. (2024): Designs a novel end-to-end framework that leverages predictive
 1312: inverse dynamics models to integrate vision and action for scalable robotic manipulation.
 1313: • VPP Hu et al. (2024): Leverages video diffusion models to generate visual representations,
 1314: addressing the limitations of traditional vision encoders in capturing temporal aspects crit-
 1315: ical for robotic manipulation.
